Cognitive Computing Research at Oak Ridge Summer 2016

Very excited to conduct research in the field of machine and cognitive learning at Oak Ridge National Laboratory this summer!


I will be working on a project titled “A cognitive Approach to Power Systems Data Analytics.” To elaborate, many automated alarm systems in power systems, which are intended to warn operators of abnormal conditions, are not effective at providing control room operators with clear, actionable warnings. Instead, operators are flooded with insignificant alarms, challenging them to identify the priority alerts to conditions that need to be addressed immediately. The objective of this project is to develop a framework that will help make sense of the great volumes of control room data – both static and dynamic – to learn, think, and recommend. We will use the concept of cognitive computing platform to help operators substantially reduce the time and resources they spend achieving alarm issue resolution.


Tune in for more this summer!

CodePhil

January 2016 - Present


CodePhil aims to teach, empower, and connect the next generation of students from disadvantaged backgrounds in Lavezares, Philippines through cross-cultural collaboration and computer-based projects, and inspire them to be peacebuilding leaders of tomorrow. We envision a program that will allow underprivileged high school students from various religious and cultural backgrounds to come together to learn the valuable and marketable skills of coding, collaborate on computer-based team projects that facilitate cross-cultural dialogue, generate interest in school through learn-by-doing activities, gain exposure to computer science-related careers, and inspire the kids to work together with people from various cultural and religious backgrounds to create solutions that can impact not only their towns but also their country and the world.

NeeroNet

January 2016 - Present


NeeroNet is an integrated and intelligent real-time water assessment model with smart analytics using machine learning and IoT to monitor water efficiently for different activities of industry, household and agriculture in urban and rural regions of developing countries by analyzing water usage and water parameters, such as pH, turbidity and contamination, at a micro level. In addition, our system will monitor rainwater harvested for groundwater replenishment when installed in channelized tanks on rooftops and alert for leakages in the distribution network when installed in the pipelines.
